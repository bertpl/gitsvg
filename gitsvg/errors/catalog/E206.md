# E206 - replaces: ids do not form a contiguous tail

## Cause

`replaces:` rule 3 — the listed commits must form a contiguous range
at the tip of the branch. You cannot squash a gap in the middle of a
branch and leave commits past it intact, and you cannot reach back
past the latest commits.

## Example

```
{"op": "branch", "name": "main"}
{"op": "commit", "branch": "main", "id": "c1", "msg": "first"}
{"op": "commit", "branch": "main", "id": "c2", "msg": "second"}
{"op": "commit", "branch": "main", "id": "c3", "msg": "third"}
{"op": "commit", "branch": "main", "id": "c4", "msg": "squash", "replaces": ["c1", "c3"]}
```

(`replaces` skips `c2` — not contiguous at the tail)

## How to fix

Either include every intermediate commit in the squash range, or use
the `remove` op + a fresh `commit` op for the more general case.
